# Product: Pearls

## Purpose

A digital implementation of the physical board game **Pearls**. Players compete in real-time to slide all 84 pearls off their wooden game boards by rolling dice and selecting target numbers.

## Users

Casual board-game players gathering remotely or in-person. One player creates a game and shares an invite code/link; others join. Ages 8+. Devices range from smartphones held in portrait to desktop monitors.

## Register

product

## Brand & Tone

- **Warm, tactile, physical.** The app should feel like you're reaching into a real wooden box of game pieces.
- **Playful but not childish.** Think quality board-game packaging: warm wood, polished brass, cream paper.
- **Confident typography.** Bold, high-contrast headings; clean body text.
- **Amber is the signature color** — derived from the warm tones of the wooden board and brass fittings.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards (white + purple gradient hero cards)
- Dark "gamer" UIs with neon accents
- Overly minimal wireframe aesthetics that feel unfinished
- Skeuomorphic wood-texture backgrounds covering the entire page

## Strategic Principles

1. The game board and dice are the hero — UI chrome should recede.
2. Every screen should work one-handed on a phone.
3. Feedback is physical: animations mimic sliding, shaking, clicking.
4. Dark mode is first-class, not an afterthought.
